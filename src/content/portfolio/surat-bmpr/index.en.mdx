## Context

Surat BMPR is a letter-correspondence and disposition application for the
Office of Roads and Spatial Planning, Central Sulawesi Province. This project
sits at the intersection of two needs typical of public-sector work: speeding
up administrative flow without losing the trail and accountability standards
that public service requires.

In the Central Sulawesi provincial context, modernising correspondence workflows
like this is a concrete step toward more responsive and well-documented
governance.

## What We Built

The Surat BMPR mobile application was built with Flutter and a Laravel backend:

- **Incoming-letter inbox**: A list of letters addressed to the user, with
  status updated along the disposition flow.
- **Multi-level disposition**: Leadership can dispose letters to staff or
  relevant divisions, with notes and follow-up instructions.
- **History and search**: Every letter has a handling trail visible to
  authorised parties — important for audits and cross-division coordination.
- **Push notifications**: Instant alerts when a new disposition arrives or
  a status changes, so follow-up is not delayed.
- **Digital signatures**: Dispositions can be signed in-app without circulating
  physical documents.

## Impact

Surat BMPR brings internal correspondence at the Office of Roads and Spatial
Planning, Central Sulawesi Province into the mobile channel, reducing
reliance on physical workflows and giving leadership the ability to disposition
letters from anywhere.

Alongside PICO Sulteng, Surat BMPR strengthens Banua Coder's portfolio of
digital solutions for the Central Sulawesi Provincial Government — at the
public-service tier (PICO) and at the internal-governance tier (Surat BMPR).
