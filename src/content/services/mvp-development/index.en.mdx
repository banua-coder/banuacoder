## Why this matters

Many good ideas get stuck in planning for too long because scope isn't controlled — every discussion adds new features, and before development even starts it feels like a massive product with no clear starting point. On the other side, many teams build without validation, spending months and significant budget only to find that what they built isn't what users actually needed.

Banua Coder's MVP development is designed to avoid both traps: moving from idea to a real, validatable product with tight scope, a realistic timeline, and focus on the features that matter most to early users.

## What we deliver

We start with a focused scoping session: mapping the problem to be solved, who the users are, and the core usage scenarios that must work well in the first version. From there, we separate what's must-have from what can wait for the next iteration.

Once scope is locked, we build an interactive prototype for the core flows, then move into development. Every component is built with a structure that enables continued development — not fast code that needs to be thrown away once validation succeeds.

## How we work

Our MVP engagements typically begin with a brief Discovery Sprint (1–2 weeks) before full development. This sprint produces: documented scope, validated user flows, and more accurate estimates. Clients can decide to proceed to the build — or adjust direction based on discovery findings — before major costs are committed.

Development runs in 2-week cycles with a demo at the end of each. This ensures clients always have visibility into progress, and there's room to course-correct before everything is finished.

## Ideal for

MVP development is the right fit for founders who want to validate an idea with a limited budget before committing to full development, or businesses going through a manual-to-digital transformation who need a clear, structured starting point.
